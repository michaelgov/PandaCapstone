Distribution of Video Gamers in the U.S from 2006-2022:

This visualization depicts the distribution of video gamers by gender, according to a survey done by the ESA. This survey was done in the U.S. from 2006 to 2022. While this distribution has slightly fluctuated over this time frame, we can see a slight increase in women playing video games. In 2006, only 38% of video gamers reported being women whereas in 2022, 48% of video gamers were reported to be women.

Most common forms of harassment while playing online video games in the US from 2019 to 2022:

Advancements in gaming technology and the availability of various games have led to widespread popularity among individuals of all ages and genders. However, the anonymity provided by online gaming can expose some players to undesirable experiences and harassment. According to a survey conducted in the United States in 2022, 76 percent of participants reported being called offensive names during gameplay. Moreover, the survey also indicated an increase in online harassment, with 86 percent of respondents experiencing it, a rise from 83 percent two years earlier.
